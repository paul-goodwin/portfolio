Buying Time

Over seven months, I worked with a client to the reimagine the business interactions between retail buyers and their wholesale suppliers. We prototyped a service oriented platform that makes retail buying visual, consolidated, and collaborative.


How did that shirt you’re wearing make its way to the store?

Every day retail buyers plan, source, and purchase of a range of products on behalf of a retailer that determines what is available to you, the consumer. The cumulative decisions of a retail buyer can account for millions of dollars in purchases per year and directly affects the success of their company.


However, before the products ever make it onto the shelves, buyers collect and manage a dizzying array of emails, attachments, phone calls, and meetings to collaborate with wholesale organizations. Our challenge was to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the retail buyer’s interaction with their wholesale supplier counterparts.


Laying the ground work.

Uncovering the Problems

I created our research field guide as a system of research activities to understand and connect a wide range of stakeholders’ needs and values. From interviews about why divisional merchandising manager care about sell-through rates, to a contextual inquiry into how products are negotiated during high profile New York meetings, to analysing what an assistant buyer does every monday before lunch.

Roles

Research and Synthesis. I lead the user research and systematically aligned our evidence, models, and insights. I wrote and coded our digital materials and co-wrote our print materials.

Business Strategy. I advocated for a high-touch, curated experience as the sustainable competitive advantage that allowed our prototypes to develop into a successful business.

Values, Benefits, and Features. I co-wrote the user stories that defined the workflow and scope of our final prototypes.

Misc. I stood at the whiteboard often and drew a lot of words, arrows, and boxes.
